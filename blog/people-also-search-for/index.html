What is “People Also Search For”?

People Also Search for (PASF) is a Google Search feature that was introduced in 2012 as part of Google’s launch of knowledge graphs. It was intended to show a collection of thumbnails for images associated with a specific search word.

PASF suggestions appear in your search results when you enter a search query, click on an organic search result, and then return to the search results page to look for more options.

The PASF box will then appear directly beneath the link you just visited. This almost seems like Google saying, “Didn’t find what you’re looking for?

These topics might be useful.” The search suggestions in the PASF box are based on URLs. The topics displayed in the box are related to the URL of the result you just clicked on, not the original search string you typed.

How do People Also Search for Work in Google Search?

‘People Also Search For’ Keywords relevant to a certain topic that is being searched are known as search terms. Google is making an effort to make it easier for users to see potentially interesting related searches while conducting topic-specific searches.

‘People Also Search For’ uses Google’s Related Searches algorithm. To determine which questions are related to the main query.

This algorithm considers several factors, including:

1. The Primary Inquiry:

The algorithm evaluates the primary query to find which queries are relevant to it.

2. The Location Of The Searcher:

The algorithm determines which searches are linked to the primary query by taking the searcher’s location into account.

3. The Device Used By The Searcher:

The algorithm determines which searches are linked to the primary query based on the searcher’s device.

4. The Search History Of The Searcher:

To decide which searches are connected to the primary query, the algorithm evaluates the search history of the user.

5. The Web Browsing History Of The Searcher:

To decide which queries are relevant to the primary query, the system evaluates the searcher’s prior web browsing activity.

6. The Content Of The SERP:

To decide which queries are relevant to the primary query, the algorithm scans the SERP’s content.

7. People Also Search For’ is Constantly Updated:

The algorithm is always running, and People Also Search For is updated in real-time to reflect the most recent related queries.

What You Need to Know About People Also Search For?

Here are the things you should know about PASF

  • Changes in the PAA SERP Positions

Most of the time, it’s natural to assume that PAA boxes appear in the same location on a SERP if and when Google triggers them.

PAA, on the other hand, differs from featured snippets, which are always displayed at the top of the SERP. PAA can also be found throughout the site.

It’s critical to understand the implications of PAA’s various positions in the SERPs because they affect the click-through rate of organic results, especially on mobile, where space is limited.

  • PAA Has the Ability to Trigger Video Results

Given that many questions begin with “What is?” It makes sense that video results would be included in “How to…” and “Why is/are?” questions.

It’s reasonable to expect Google to continue testing this until most of the keywords that show video results in the SERPs also trigger videos in the PAA listings.

This means you should spend time optimizing your YouTube and video results if you want to appear in the PAAs.

  • PAA Repeats and Stimulates Featured Snippets Frequently

The majority of PAA questions result in featured snippets. For different keywords, the same PAA question and answer may appear. The same answers and listings that appear in the PAA for a specific inquiry may also appear for other questions triggered by PAAs.

What are the Differences between People Also Search for and Related Searches?

There are a few significant differences between Related Searches and People Also Search For.

  1. To begin, Related Searches is a feature at the bottom of the SERP, whereas Google Also Search for is a People Also Ask (PAA) box that appears on the SERP after a user clicks on a result and then returns to the SERP.
  2. People Also Search For displays the number of searches for each query, whereas Related Searches only displays a list of related queries.
  3. People Also Search For is specific to the main query that was entered, whereas Related Searches includes results for unrelated queries.
  4. People Also Search For is updated in real-time, whereas Related Searches is updated on a regular basis.

How to Manage Duplicate PASF Topics?

With so many PASF topics to choose from, it can be difficult to know where to focus and how to handle duplicate PASF topics.

Here are three things you should do:

1. There is Only One Landing Page For Related Queries:

The next thing is to create a single landing page where you can group all of your relevant links. In this manner, you inform Google that your content is not duplicated.

2. Use Canonical Tags:

You can use canonical tags to prevent duplicate PASF discussions. Canonical tags are used in SEO to inform Google which version of a page you want to see in search results, to combine link equity from duplicate pages, and to make your website easier for crawlers and indexers to navigate.

3. User Redirect:

You can redirect users to more relevant content from your landing page. As a result, Google will view your website as a resource for that certain niche, which will benefit SEO.
